I am currently a Research Manager at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore, working under the supervision of Prof. Adams Wai-Kin Kong. My current research is part of a joint initiative with Interpol, with a focus on video and temporal understanding. I received my Master's degree from the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi during which I had the privilege to intern at Microsoft Research. I earned my Bachelor’s degree in Information Technology from the National Institute of Technology, Srinagar.
Outside research, I enjoy badminton and swimming. I am also passionate about creating positive social impact, which led me to initiate Ralith Milith, an anti-drug society in Kashmir and cofounded Gaash Lab to mentor students. When I am not training models, I am usually training my badminton reflexes.

Research

Community

    In my academic journey, I transitioned into a research-focused lifestyle, driven by a deep curiosity to explore Computer Vision and Medical Imaging. My path has been dynamic, with a domain shift from IoT to robotics, reflecting a continuous pursuit of learning and innovation. I am an advocate for open-source contributions, as they foster collective growth and help both educators and learners critically engage with the wealth of information available online.
    With this notion in mind, I co-founded GAASH Research Lab, where I currently mentor B-Tech and Master's students. Through this initiative, we aim to support collaborative research and create opportunities for students interested in AI, computer vision, and related areas.
    The guidance and support of my academic mentors and peers have been pivotal in shaping my early career, and their influence continues to inspire me. Building on this foundation, I am actively mentoring undergraduate and master’s students in computer vision, and I look forward to supporting more learners in the future. I particularly encourage students with diverse backgrounds or unconventional academic paths, similar to my own, to connect and explore opportunities for growth and research.
